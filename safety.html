Community & Safety

Last updated: June 15, 2026

Bazmi is built for real conversation. These guidelines keep our rooms welcoming for everyone. They apply to everything you do on Bazmi — your voice, chat, profile, room name, and any content you share. Breaking them can lead to content removal, mutes, kicks, bans, or account termination.

Be respectful

  • Treat others the way you'd want to be treated. Healthy debate is welcome; harassment is not.
  • No bullying, threats, intimidation, or coordinated harassment.
  • No hate speech or attacks based on race, ethnicity, religion, nationality, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or any protected characteristic.

Zero tolerance

The following lead to immediate action and, where required, reporting to authorities:

  • Child safety: any sexual content involving minors, grooming, or endangerment of a child.
  • Sexual content & nudity in voice, profiles, or room content.
  • Violence & extremism: threats, incitement, or promotion of terrorism or violent groups.
  • Illegal activity: drugs, weapons trafficking, fraud, scams, or sale of stolen goods.
  • Self-harm: encouraging or glorifying suicide or self-harm. (If you're struggling, please reach out to local emergency services or a crisis line.)

Protect privacy

  • Don't share someone's private information without consent (doxxing).
  • Don't record or redistribute others' audio without an appropriate, lawful basis and consent.
  • Remember rooms are live and may be public — avoid sharing your own sensitive details on the mic.

Keep it authentic

  • No impersonation of people, brands, or Bazmi staff.
  • No spam, mass-inviting, fake engagement, or manipulation of levels, gifts, or rankings.
  • No buying, selling, or transferring accounts.

For room owners & admins

If you host or moderate a room, you help set its tone. Use the tools available to you:

  • Mute disruptive speakers, lock seats, and manage who's on the mic.
  • Kick to remove someone (kicked users are added to the room's blacklist and can be un-kicked later).
  • Ban for serious or repeat violations.
  • Add trusted admins with the permissions you choose.

Owners are responsible for keeping their rooms within these guidelines.

Reporting & blocking

If you see something that breaks these rules:

  • Use the in-app report option on a user, room, or message.
  • Block users you don't want to interact with.
  • For urgent safety concerns, email support@bazmivoicechat.com.

Enforcement

Depending on severity and history, we may remove content, warn, mute, kick, ban, suspend, or permanently terminate accounts, and remove associated virtual items. Some violations are reported to law enforcement. We aim to be fair, but we prioritize the safety of our community.

A note on website previews: public rooms may offer a short, listen-only preview on our website. By hosting or speaking in a public room, you understand this audio may be heard by others, including website visitors. Hosts who prefer privacy can use room locks and seat controls.
